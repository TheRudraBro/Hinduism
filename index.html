History Of Hinduism

Hinduism is the world’s oldest religion, which began over 5,000 years ago in ancient India.
It has no single founder and developed from the spiritual traditions of the Indus Valley Civilization.
The main holy books are the Vedas, Upanishads, Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Bhagavad Gita.
Hindus believe in one supreme reality called Brahman, and in key ideas such as Dharma (righteous duty),
Karma (actions and their results), Samsara (rebirth), and Moksha (freedom from rebirth).
Major gods include Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Lakshmi, Saraswati, and Durga. Hindus worship through
puja, meditation, and yoga, and celebrate festivals like Diwali, Holi, and Durga Puja.
Hinduism teaches peace, tolerance, and spiritual growth, guiding people to live
righteously and seek union with the divine.

Biggest Festivals

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, celebrates the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil.

Navaratri

Navaratri is a nine-night festival honoring the goddess Durga and her victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura.
