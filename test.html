About Hinduism

Hinduism, known as Sanatan Dharma, developed over thousands of years in ancient India. It has no single founder and is guided by scriptures like the Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita and Puranas. Core beliefs include Dharma (duty), Karma (action), Moksha (liberation) and reincarnation.

Deities

Major gods and goddesses, with short descriptions and common mantras.

Lord Krishna

The eighth avatar of Vishnu; teacher of the Bhagavad Gita — symbol of love and wisdom.

Mantra: "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"

Lord Shiva

The destroyer and transformer; worshipped as the supreme yogi and meditator.

Mantra: "Om Namah Shivaya"

Goddess Durga

Divine mother and protector — destroys evil and restores Dharma.

Mantra: "Om Dum Durgayei Namaha"

Lord Ganesha

Remover of obstacles, patron of arts and sciences; worshipped at the start of new ventures.

Mantra: "Om Gam Ganapataye Namaha"

Lord Vishnu

The preserver — protects dharma and appears in many avatars to restore balance.

Mantra: "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"

Festivals

Important Hindu festivals with short descriptions and meanings.

Durga Puja

Celebrates Goddess Durga’s victory over Mahishasura; a time of devotion, art and community gatherings.

Krishna Janmashtami

Marks the birth of Lord Krishna; celebrated with singing, dancing and midnight bhajans.

Diwali

Festival of Lights symbolizing the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Rath Yatra

Chariot festival (notably in Puri) honoring the deities when they move publicly to bless devotees.

Saraswati Puja

Honors Goddess Saraswati, the deity of knowledge, arts and learning.

Scriptures & Teachings

Key writings and short passages that guide practice and philosophy in Hinduism.

Bhagavad Gita

A dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna about duty, action and devotion.

"You have the right to work, but not to the fruits of your actions." — Gita 2.47

The Vedas

The oldest corpus of sacred hymns and ritual knowledge that underpins Hindu traditions.

"Truth is one; sages call it by different names."

Upanishads

Mystical and philosophical texts that explore Atman (self) and Brahman (ultimate reality).

"The Self is not born, nor does it die; it is eternal and indestructible."

Famous Temples

A few notable temples from India and Bangladesh with short histories and locations.

Jagannath Temple, Puri (India)

One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites. Famous for the annual Rath Yatra (chariot festival).

Dakshineswar Kali Temple (India)

Famous Kali temple near Kolkata; known for spiritual gatherings and Ramakrishna's association.
