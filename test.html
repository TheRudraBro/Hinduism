About Hinduism

Hinduism, known as Sanatan Dharma, is the world’s oldest religion. It originated thousands of years ago in ancient India and has no single founder. It teaches the principles of Dharma (righteousness), Karma (action), and Moksha (liberation). Hinduism embraces peace, truth, devotion, and respect for all living beings.

Divine Deities

Lord Krishna

The eighth incarnation of Lord Vishnu, symbol of love, wisdom, and divine joy.

Lord Shiva

The destroyer and transformer, representing meditation, power, and rebirth.

Goddess Durga

The goddess of strength and protection who destroys evil and brings harmony.

Hindu Festivals

Hindu festivals celebrate faith, joy, and devotion throughout the year.

Diwali

Festival of Lights celebrating the victory of good over evil.

Janmashtami

Marks the birth of Lord Krishna, celebrated with devotion and joy.

Durga Puja

A festival honoring Goddess Durga’s victory over the demon Mahishasura.

Scriptures & Teachings

“You have the right to work, but not to the fruits of your action.” — Bhagavad Gita 2:47

The Bhagavad Gita, Vedas, and Upanishads teach humanity to live in truth, harmony, and devotion. These sacred scriptures guide people toward self-realization and inner peace.
